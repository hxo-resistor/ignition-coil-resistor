✅ Certification & Quality
7. Do you have AEC-Q200 certification?+

Yes. IG-C and IG-F series have AEC-Q200 Standard Grade certification. IG-S series has Full Grade certification (all tests). Third-party test reports available.

8. What does AEC-Q200 certification mean for ignition coil resistors?+

AEC-Q200 is the Automotive Electronics Council passive component stress test qualification standard. It is the entry requirement for automotive OE market components.

Passing AEC-Q200 means the resistor has undergone 20+ rigorous stress tests, including:

  • High-temperature life test (125°C~155°C, 1000 hours)
  • Temperature cycling (-55°C~+155°C, 1000 cycles)
  • Humidity bias test (85°C/85%RH, 1000 hours)
  • Vibration/shock test
  • Solderability/heat resistance

Without certification, resistors may experience resistance drift, open circuits, or insulation failure in automotive environments. HXO IG-C/IG-F have Standard Grade, IG-S has Full Grade certification.

9. What quality system certifications does your factory have?+

Products are AEC-Q200 certified. Factory quality management system complies with ISO 9001. COA (Certificate of Analysis), batch traceability codes, and RoHS/REACH compliance declarations available on request.

10. What is the shelf life of the resistors?+

Under normal storage conditions (temperature -10°C~40°C, humidity ≤70%RH), shelf life is 2 years. Store in original packaging, avoid high temperature and humidity.

  • Automotive: Universal replacement parts, aftermarket
  • Compatible resistance: Secondary 4.7kΩ~10kΩ, primary 0.5Ω~1.5Ω
13. What's the difference between 4.7kΩ, 5kΩ, and 10kΩ?+

All are secondary suppressor resistors, differing in EMI suppression strength:

  • 4.7kΩ: Universal, moderate EMI suppression, least ignition energy loss
  • 5kΩ~6.8kΩ: Most common range, balancing EMI suppression and ignition energy
  • 10kΩ: Strong EMI suppression, for sensitive onboard electronics

Higher resistance = stronger EMI suppression but slightly more ignition energy loss. Most motorcycle applications recommend 4.7kΩ~5.1kΩ.
