4.7kΩ vs 10kΩ Ignition Coil Suppressor Resistor: Which One Should You Choose?

HXO Resistor · Technical Guide · July 2026

This is one of the most frequently asked questions by motorcycle mechanics and parts buyers worldwide. 4.7kΩ and 10kΩ suppressor resistors look identical, have the same pin configuration, and similar pricing — but can you use them interchangeably?

Short answer: In most cases yes, but with conditions. Using the wrong value can affect engine performance, and in severe cases, cause ECU misreading or insufficient ignition energy.

Core Differences Between 4.7kΩ and 10kΩ

Comparison4.7kΩ10kΩ
EMI SuppressionModerate, suppresses 60%~70% of HF oscillationStrong, suppresses 80%~90%
Ignition Energy LossApprox. 5%~8%Approx. 10%~15%
Best ForCarburetor/EFI motorcycles, general automotiveEFI vehicles, high compression, ECU-equipped models
Risk if too high—Insufficient ignition energy at high RPM, potential misfire
Risk if too lowInsufficient EMI suppression, ECU/audio interference—

When Can You Use Them Interchangeably?

For temporary replacement only, if these conditions are met:

  • Carburetor motorcycles: No ECU, less sensitive to EMI. 4.7kΩ and 10kΩ are interchangeable — differences are barely noticeable
  • EFI motorcycles under 250cc: 4.7kΩ is preferred. 10kΩ can be used as a temporary substitute, but monitor idle stability

When NOT to Substitute

  • Large displacement EFI (400cc+): Replacing 4.7kΩ with 10kΩ may cause high-RPM misfire due to excessive ignition energy loss
  • Models specified for 10kΩ: Using 4.7kΩ will cause excessive EMI interference, affecting oxygen sensor signals and ECU air-fuel ratio readings
  • Modern CAN-bus vehicles: Extremely sensitive to EMI. Must use OEM-specified resistance values

⚡ Quick Identification Method: Check the color coding on the original spark plug cap resistor. Japanese models (Honda/Yamaha/Suzuki) typically use 4.7kΩ (green or blue marking), European models (Piaggio/Peugeot) use 5kΩ~10kΩ (red or yellow marking). When in doubt, remove and measure the original resistance with a multimeter.

Selection Recommendations

  • Universal replacement: Choose 4.7kΩ — covers the vast majority of motorcycle applications
  • EMI-sensitive environments (car audio/communication equipment): Choose 10kΩ for stronger suppression
  • Large displacement/high RPM: Choose 5kΩ~6.8kΩ to balance EMI suppression and ignition energy
  • When unsure: Use the original OEM resistance value, or contact us for free selection advice
